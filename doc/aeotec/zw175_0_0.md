# ZW175 Smart Switch 7
This describes the Z-Wave device *ZW175*, manufactured by *Aeotec Limited* with the thing type UID of ```aeotec_zw175_00_000```.

The device is in the category of *Power Outlet*, defining Small devices to be plugged into a power socket in a wall which stick there.

![ZW175 product image](https://opensmarthouse.org/zwavedatabase/1134/image/)


The ZW175 supports routing. This allows the device to communicate using other routing enabled devices as intermediate routers.  This device is also able to participate in the routing of data between other devices in the mesh network.

## Overview

<p&gtAeotec Smart Switch 7 (SS7) has been crafted to power connected lighting using SmartStart, S2 framework, and Z-Wave Plus. It is powered by Aeotec’s S2 technology. </p&gt <ul&gt<li&gtSmart Switch 7 is intended for indoor use in dry locations only. Do not use in damp, moist, and/or wet locations.</li&gt <li&gtMax-amperage: 15 amps for ZW175-A</li&gt <li&gtMay-amperage: 10 amps for ZW175-C</li&gt </ul&gt

### Inclusion Information

<p&gtGetting your Smart Switch up and running is as simple as plugging it into a wall socket and adding it to your Z-Wave network.</p&gt <ol&gt<li&gtSet your Z-Wave Controller into its 'Add Device' mode in order to add the product into your Z-Wave system. Refer to the Controller's manual if you are unsure of how to perform this step.</li&gt <li&gtPower your Smart Switch 7 to any outlet; its LED will be breathing a blue light if it is ready to be paired.</li&gt <li&gtClick Action Button 2 times slowly to set your Smart Switch 7 into pair mode.</li&gt <li&gtIf your Z-Wave Controller supports S2 encryption, enter the first 5 digits of DSK into your Controller's interface if it is requested. The DSK is printed on its Smart Switch 7.<br /&gta) If pairing succeeds, it will become a bright blue light for 2 seconds. Smart Switch 7 is now a part of your Z-Wave home control system. You can configure it and its automation via your Z-Wave system; please refer to your software's user guide for precise instructions.<br /&gtb) If pairing fails, it will light a solid bright red light for 2 seconds and then return to a breathing blue light; repeat steps 1 to 4 if this happens.</li&gt </ol&gt

### Exclusion Information

<p&gtYour Smart Switch can be removed from your Z-Wave network at any time. You’ll need to use your Z-Wave network’s main controller to do this and the following instructions will tell you how to do this using Aeotec by Aeon Labs’ Z-Stick or Minimote controller.<br /&gt<br /&gtIf you are using other products as your main Z-Wave controller, please refer to the part of their respective manuals that tells you how to remove devices from your network.</p&gt <p&gtIf you're using an existing gateway:</p&gt <ol&gt<li&gtSet your Z-Wave Controller into its 'Remove Device' / 'Unpair Device' mode in order to remove the product from your Z-Wave system. Refer to the Controller's manual if you are unsure of how to perform this step.</li&gt <li&gtMake sure the product is powered. If not, plug it into a wall socket and power it on.</li&gt <li&gtClick Action Button 2 times quickly; it will bright violet light up to 2s.<br /&gta. If Removing fails, it will bright red light for 2 seconds and then turn back to Load Indicator Mode; repeat steps 1 to 3. Contact us for further support if needed.<br /&gtb. If Removing succeeds, it will become breathing blue light. Smart Switch 7 is removed from the Z-Wave network successfully and is ready to be paired again.</li&gt </ol&gt

## Association Groups

Association groups allow the device to send unsolicited reports to the controller, or other devices in the network. Using association groups can allow you to eliminate polling, providing instant feedback of a device state change without unnecessary network traffic.

The ZW175 supports 1 association group.

load notification type (lifeline)
